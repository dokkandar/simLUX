Handling the mesh

Everything DIALux and its peers hand to the light calc is the same thing: a watertight, per-surface tessellated mesh with reflectance. The Factory already makes solids — but as a flat, anonymous triangle soup. The whole of Steps 1–2 is one transformation across a single seam. Get this right and the rest of the roadmap is just consuming it — which is exactly the instinct: once the mesh is fixed, the other parts fall into place.

The one hard part

The soup carries a normal per triangle but no identity. Welding it into an indexed mesh, walking coplanar adjacency into faces, and classifying each face by its normal is what turns a blob into a room the calc understands. Sub-steps 01 · 03 · 06 · 07 are the keystone; every later roadmap step reuses their output rather than reinventing it.

STEP 1

Per-surface mesh identity

goal: surfaces become addressable objects
01 keystone

Weld soup → indexed vertices

Deduplicate positions by a quantised key (~1e-5 m) into a shared vertex buffer with adjacency. This is also the watertight foundation — shared edges stop being seams.

SolidMesh.positionsVertexTriangle
proof A box's 36 soup points collapse to 8 verts / 12 tris.
02

Per-triangle face normal

Take the csgrs normal (constant across a tri) or recompute (b−a)×(c−a). This is the grouping and classification key that Steps 3–4 later lean on for free.

SolidMesh.normalsglam::Vec3::cross
proof A box yields 6 distinct normals, ±X / ±Y / ±Z.
03 keystone

Partition into coplanar faces

Reuse the existing adjacency walk over the welded mesh to group triangles into connected coplanar regions — one region = one surface.

coplanar_face(positions, start)
proof A box partitions into exactly 6 faces of 2 tris each.
04

Stamp SurfaceId + provenance

Each face records { id, feature_id, normal, centroid, tris }, tying the surface back to the Feature it came from so it stays addressable across re-eval.

Feature.idstruct Surface
proof Two features → surfaces carry distinct feature ids.
05

Pick → highlight the face

Ray-pick a triangle (reuse the Factory's existing pick), look up its Surface, highlight all its triangles. Closes Step 1 and pre-builds the Step 8 material-picker interaction.

factory.pickcad_solid::ray_triangle
proof Click a wall → its two triangles light up together.
STEP 2

Seam into the calc — uniform reflectance

goal: "Factory is the room" · Phase 0
06 keystone

Surface → cad_light::Mesh

One face becomes one cad_light::Mesh — its welded vertices + triangles, with material set to a single uniform default id. Per-class reflectance is deliberately deferred to Step 4.

Mesh { vertices, triangles, material }
proof One face → one Mesh; vertices are welded, not soup.
07 keystone

Assemble the scene Vec<Mesh>

Map every Surface of every Feature into Vec<cad_light::Mesh> — the whole Factory model as the calc's scene input, structurally identical to what box_room() hand-builds today.

Vec<cad_light::Mesh>
proof A box → Vec<Mesh> of len 6, matching box_room().
08

Wire the calc + a default scene

A "Calculate" action builds the meshes, a CalcPlane from the model bounds at 0.8 m, default_materials(), one test luminaire + IES, and calls the engine.

calculate(…) -> LuxGridCalcPlanedefault_materials()
proof "Calculate" returns a populated LuxGrid.
09

Cache + dirty gate

Rebuild Vec<Mesh> only when factory.cached changes — mirror the existing dirty flag. Welding + csgrs aren't cheap; never reconvert per-frame.

factory.cacheddirty
proof Idle frames perform zero conversions.
10 keystone

Proof harness — the box occludes

Unit test: box → convert → calculate → the grid is non-zero and a sensor beneath the box is darker than an open one. This is verbatim the roadmap's Step-2 proof — light bounces off the room the Factory made.

#[test]LuxGrid
phase 0 Sensor under box < open sensor; grid > 0. Done.
